Tower Takeover

August 2019 - April 2020

The challenge for the 2019-2020 VEX Robotics season was Tower Takeover, in which robots must stack cubes in the corners of the playing field and multiply the values of specific color cubes by scoring them in towers. For this challenge, I lead a group of 12 members to design, build, and program a robot to both stack cubes with a long rack and lift cubes into towers to multiply the color values. Through months of iterations and testing, our team found a most effective design, depicted in the pictures above, where two conveyer belt rollers intake cubes and can also be raised via a lifting mechanism to score cubes in elevated towers. One interesting mechanism we built was a "cube lock", which held cubes in the storage rack even if the rollers were lifted to score in towers. Above is a video of a scrimmage between various teams at Dublin High School the day before the California State Championship (unfortunately cancelled last minute). In the video, I am in the far left controlling the robot with black plastic plates on the side and the 5327V license plate. Not only do we have to build and program the robot to have the most efficient stacking design, but we also devised many strategies during the driver control and autonomous period to perform as well as possible.

Turning Point

August 2018 - April 2019

The challenge for the 2018-2019 VEX Robotics season was Turning Point, where robots must shoot balls to toggle flags, flip caps, and climb platforms. For this challenge, I worked with a team of 12 members to design, build, and program a robot to launch balls with a catapult, manipulate caps with a horizontal roller, and climb the platforms. Through months of iterations and testing, our team tuned and optimized a catapult to launch two balls at once in order to hit two flags at once. The balls are intaked into the robot with rubber band rollers and are launched using a variety of sensors for control. The video above shows our 15 second autonomous period that we preprogrammed for the California State Championship. Here, the robot relies purly on sensors to navigate around the field and shoot balls.

In The Zone

August 2017 - April 2018

The challenge for the 2017-2018 VEX Robotics season was In The Zone, where robots must stack cones on mobile goals to score in a variety of corner zones. For this challenge, I worked with a team of 14 members to design, build, and program a robot that uses horizontal rollers to collect cones and a reverse double four bar lift to stack them. Through months of iterations and testing, our team fine tuned our lift to be as fast, frictionless, and optimally tensioned as possible to have speedy stacking during competition matches. The video above shows our driver load practice in preparation for the California State Championship. Here, the robot is able to stack 12 cones within 18 seconds, which was quite fast compared to other teams in Northern California.
